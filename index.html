Trail miles ahead

Filters the lists below to what’s ahead of you in your current direction of travel — set both to plan a day’s ride. A negative number looks behind you, which is where Prev steps. Needs your location.

Trail mile range

TM is trail mile — the fixed milepost a place sits at, measured along the route from the NYC end. Unlike the band above it, this is absolute rather than relative to you, so it works without your location.

Outlook

Forecast from the US National Weather Service (api.weather.gov), read where you’d be at 12 mph rather than where you are now. Wind is split into the part you ride into and the part crossing you. NWS hourly runs about six days out, which is the ceiling on days here.

Average riding speed

Moving average over a day, not what the computer flashes on a downhill — 10 to 13 is honest for a loaded touring bike. It decides where you’d be at each forecast hour, so the weather layer and the Outlook both hang off it.

Your own layers

Anything you add turns up in the map\u2019s layers control, off until you switch it on, and can be dragged into whatever order you want it drawn in. An ArcGIS URL is a single layer\u2019s endpoint \u2014 the one ending in a number. It\u2019s fetched over New York, up to 2,000 features, and stored as the URL so it stays current. A GPX is stored as its points, since there\u2019s nowhere to fetch it back from.
